Client Portal

A modular, widget-based wealth management portal giving advisors and their clients a single, cohesive view of their entire financial lives — tested with real users and anchored in a scalable design system.

The Problem

One view for an entire financial life

Advisors needed a way to show their value to clients in real time — but the existing experience was fragmented across accounts, lacked customization, and made it difficult to surface the insights that matter most.

The challenge was designing a portal that felt personalized for each client, while remaining flexible and maintainable across an enterprise-scale platform.

Approach

Research-led, system-first

I grounded the design in competitive analysis of modern financial platforms, validated key decisions with moderated user testing, and ensured every component contributed back to the design system.

01

The Work

From testing insights to shipped product

Each section of the portal was shaped by direct user feedback. Testing surfaced the gaps; design closed them.

Dashboard · Widget Experience
Contextual widget placement with preview

User testing revealed that advisors struggled to understand what a widget would look like before placing it on the dashboard. We tested two models — drag and drop vs. a curated widget list — and found that a list with contextual descriptions and a live preview reduced errors and increased confidence. The widget experience was later added to the design system and used by other designers across different products.

Realized Gain/Loss
Column rearrangement based on advisor feedback

Testing the realized gain/loss page with advisors revealed that the existing column order didn't match how they mentally process client data. We rearranged the columns based on their feedback — prioritizing the fields they reach for first — making the page faster to scan and act on.

Design System
Dark mode color standards & accessibility

I defined the dark mode information colors for the design system, ensuring all data visualizations and financial indicators met accessibility contrast standards. These tokens were documented and handed off to engineering with clear usage guidelines.

Responsive Guidelines
7–10 breakpoints for engineering

I defined the responsive guidelines for the portal, specifying 7–10 breakpoints that covered everything from widescreen advisor workstations to tablet-sized client views. This gave engineering a clear, unambiguous reference and reduced back-and-forth during implementation.
