UX Maturity

Understanding where we are to know where to go

Understanding the organization's current level of UX maturity provided the insights needed to take targeted steps toward advancing to the next stage.

The examples below highlight key actions implemented by leadership to drive this growth — from building team structure to socializing design's value across the organization.

Team Composition

Right skills, right place, right time

Understanding specific skills the team has and utilizing them strategically to align with product needs. This allowed the team to develop a method to specialize in design roles — moving from generalists to a team of focused contributors.

Design Process

Turning creative energy into a predictable, scalable discipline

A clear design process ensures quality and alignment across every product surface. We defined shared rituals, decision checkpoints, and artifact standards so the team could move fast without losing coherence.

Design Values

Turning values into action

Culture doesn't happen by accident. These five values shaped how we approached every project, every review, and every conversation.

01
Empathy
Why
Understanding teammates and users fosters collaboration and helps create meaningful designs.
How
Active listening, open communication, and user-centered approaches in every decision.
02
Collaboration
Why
Great outcomes are often a result of diverse perspectives and team effort.
How
Inclusive brainstorming, shared goals, and cross-functional teamwork at every stage.
03
Creativity & Innovation
Why
Staying ahead in design means constantly challenging the status quo.
How
Space for experimentation, celebrating unconventional thinking, and supporting risk-taking.
04
Ownership Mindset
Why
A culture of responsibility and adaptability drives long-term success and innovation.
How
Embrace experimentation, celebrate learning from mistakes, and invest in growth.
05
Focus on Impact
Why
Teams are more motivated when their efforts create meaningful value for users and the business.
How
Connect daily work to broader goals, communicate outcomes regularly, and celebrate wins.
