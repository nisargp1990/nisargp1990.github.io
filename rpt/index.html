The Problem

Subscriptions accumulate, and banks had no way to help

Most consumers have no clear picture of what they are paying for each month. Streaming services, gym memberships, and forgotten trials quietly drain accounts with no central place to track them.

  • No single view of all recurring charges across aggregated accounts
  • Unused subscriptions going unnoticed for months
  • No easy path to cancel or modify payments from within the bank
  • Banks missing an opportunity to turn savings awareness into deposits or investments

Business Need

Give banks a tool that helps customers eliminate wasted spend and redirects those savings into the broader financial product suite.

Strategy

Design from the data up

Before jumping into visuals, I spent time with engineering to understand what data was actually available through the API. That foundation shaped everything, from how merchants were identified (with their logos) to how cancellation flows were structured, and gave the team clear guardrails to explore concepts quickly without designing into dead ends.

Outcomes

A tool that turns awareness into action

The Recurring Payments Tracker gave customers full visibility into their recurring charges for the first time, and created a natural bridge back into the broader PFM suite. The tool opened a direct path to redirect identified savings into adjacent financial applications like a bank's Savings product — turning subscription awareness into measurable deposit opportunity.
